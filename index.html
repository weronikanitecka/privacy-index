How We Score

Each privacy policy is evaluated against the seven core principles of the GDPR (Regulation EU 2016/679). Each principle is scored on a 0–10 scale. The composite score is the arithmetic mean of all available principle scores.

GDPR Principle What we evaluate

Risk Classification

✓ Safe — 7.5 to 10.0

Policy demonstrates strong GDPR compliance across all seven principles. Users can engage with reasonable confidence.

⚠ Review Required — 6.0 to 7.4

Policy is partially compliant but contains notable gaps. Users should review specific data categories before sharing sensitive information.

✗ High Risk — below 6.0

Policy shows significant deficiencies in one or more core GDPR principles. Users face material risk of their data being used in ways they did not meaningfully consent to.

The DPA Enforcement Layer

From Edition 2 onwards, each score will be cross-referenced with the company's documented regulatory enforcement history. A company may write a compliant policy while practising differently — DPA fines are the strongest available signal of that gap.

Enforcement data is sourced from the CMS GDPR Enforcement Tracker, national DPA publications (CNIL, ICO, UODO, Garante), and EDPB public registers. Only publicly documented fines are included.

Limitations

This analysis evaluates privacy policies as written documents. It does not assess actual company behaviour, technical implementation of data practices, or undisclosed data flows. Scores are AI-assisted analyses using Guardatum's GDPR evaluation framework and should not be construed as legal advice.

Guardatum's data asset is intelligence about companies, not about users. This is the ethical foundation of the business model. All enforcement data used in this index is publicly available and cited to its source.
